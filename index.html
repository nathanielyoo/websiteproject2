The Grip

Your control over the golf club is dictated by the quality of your grip. An improper golf grip is extremely prevalent throughout beginner golfers and can negatively impact consistency and speed, with both the golf swing and ball flight. Throughout the golf swing, your hands should remain gripping the golf club without slippage or release. For right-handed players, the majority of grip pressure should be provided by the right hand's middle and ring finger and the left hand's last 3 fingers (middle, ring, and pinky finger), and vice versa for left-handed players.

Alignment

Alignment is an extremely critical aspect of the golf setup. Your body and club-face must be correctly aligned to your target in order for the golf ball to travel on the desired path. Without proper alignment, the hands, arms, head, shoulders, hips, knees, and feet will attempt to compensate for the inaccuracy, leading to inconsistency. Backswing

The backswing is an essential component in the golf swing. It accumulates energy by creating distance away from the ball, which sets the golf club in a great position to hit the ball accurately, and assists in building speed into impact. Although only the impact and follow-through of the golf swing affect the flight of the ball, the golf club's position at the peak of your backswing determines the ease with which you can get into a strong impact position. Furthermore, your backswing significantly affects your swing plane throughout the downswing and follow-through.
